What are Projects?

Projects are a collaborative effort between FBLA members that range in topics and teach FBLA's principles.


Every year, the American Enterprise Project aims to increase the understanding and support of the American Enterprise System, or the U.S economic system of free enterprise, within our local community. During the 2021-22 school year, we are hosting financial literacy educational programs for Elementary and Middle School students across the Bay Area - spanning from our hometown, Cupertino, to Oakland and San Francisco. These programs aim to spark interest in finance amongst our younger community while also introducing the pillars of free enterprise.


Past Project Awards:

During the 2018-19 school year, the last competitive FBLA season that Cupertino High had partaken in, our former American Enterprise Project Chair, Zoe Liau, placed 3rd in the State and 6th in the nation. This is the highest-ranked AEP that Cupertino High has ever had, and, now, we’ll have the opportunity to compete once again during the 2021-22 season.



Cupertino FBLA Middle Level takes place in Lawson and Hyde Middle School, where participants help middle schoolers sharpen their communication, leadership, and teamwork skills, preparing them for high school and the workplace before many of their peers. Through participating in Middle Level, members can strengthen their leadership and teaching skills while simultaneously practicing their business skills, making being a part of the Cupertino FBLA Middle Level committee an extremely enriching experience. Through workshops, programs, simulations, and external events that connect Middle Level to the wider FBLA community, Tino FBLA Middle Level hopes to prepare middle school students for the future, both in FBLA and beyond. Feel free to reach out to Anusha Singhai, the External Projects Chair, with any questions!



By participating in Individual Projects, members get the chance to strengthen business-related skills as well as explore a wide variety of topics while making a difference in their community. 2021-2022 Partner: Neolth - https://www.neolth.com/ Partnership with Business is a project in which each year, Cupertino FBLA forms a mutually beneficial partnership with a local company. The goal is to positively impact the partner company, for example through expanding their user base and enhancing products/services -- while simultaneously providing students with the opportunity to learn about business in a real-world environment. During the 2021-2022 school year, Cupertino FBLA is partnering with the tech startup company, Neolth, to make a meaningful impact in the realm of mental health and wellness.


Past Project Awards:

Notably in the 2017-2018 school year, Cupertino FBLA’s Partnership with Business project placed 2nd at the state conference and advanced to the national level. The partner business, BrightHeart Yoga Studio, saw an $11,000 net increase in revenue from their partnership with our committee.
